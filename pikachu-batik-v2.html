The story behind the cards

Where Pokémon
meets heritage

In 2024, The Pokémon Company launched Pikachu's Indonesia Journey — an ambitious, multi-city campaign born from a partnership with Garuda Indonesia Airlines. The campaign was conceived under the broader Pokémon Air Adventures framework, but adapted entirely for Indonesian culture and audiences.

At its heart was a simple but powerful idea: dress Pikachu in batik. Not a generic "Asian" motif — but a carefully crafted series of five distinct batik patterns, each featuring a different Pokémon species woven into traditional Javanese design language.

"Batik is an art and tradition recognized in 2009 as part of Indonesia's Intangible Cultural Heritage by UNESCO — and now, for the first time, it wears a Pikachu."

The campaign unfolded across Indonesian cities throughout 2024, with each location receiving an exclusive variant of the Pikachu Berkemeja Batik card. The cards were never distributed internationally and were not available through any online Pokémon store. The only way to obtain them was to be physically present at the events — or to source them from Indonesia afterward.

The campaign continues in 2025, with events confirmed in Palembang, Semarang, Bandung, Bekasi, and Medan — making this an ongoing series, not a one-time release.

Cultural context

Batik — the art
behind the cards

Batik is a wax-resist dyeing technique applied to cloth, originating from Java, Indonesia. In 2009, UNESCO added batik to its Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity. Each motif carries a specific meaning — social status, spiritual protection, or ceremonial purpose. The Pokémon Company worked with batik artisans to create patterns that authentically blend Pokémon characters with traditional Javanese motif language.

The five motifs of the Indonesia Journey

⚡

Pikachu & Poké Ball

The debut motif. Circular forms echoing traditional kawung patterns, reinterpreted with Poké Balls. Chic and formal — suitable for everyday batik wear.

🌀

Xatu

Lively yet formal palette. The Mystic Pokémon Xatu is woven into the design using blues and greens — colors associated with spirituality in Javanese tradition.

⭐

Staryu

Muted brown earth tones — the most classical batik palette. Staryu's bright red core creates a focal accent point that mirrors traditional parang motif accents.

🌸

Pikachu & Flowers

A departure from the earthy palette — bright background with geometric florals. Inspired by the Batik Pekalongan tradition, known for its vivid colors and floral abundance.

🐟

Magikarp

White and orange dominate, reflecting Magikarp's signature colors. Wave and water elements create a dynamic, elegant design — a motif of transformation and perseverance.

How batik is made — the 5 traditional steps

01

Diketeli

Preparing materials. Soaking the base cloth overnight to ready it for wax application.

02

Nyanting

Tracing patterns. Covering the design with hot wax using a canting tool — to prevent dyeing.

03

Nyelup

Dyeing. Dipping the cloth in dye liquid to set the colors.

04

Nglorod

Removing wax by washing the cloth in hot water mixed with soda ash solution.

05

Mepe

Drying the wet cloth under direct sunlight to fix colors and finish the textile.

Why 101/SV-P commands a premium

The Bali version was the first card distributed across multiple channels (TCG Academia, pop-up store, Gym stores) and includes the highest-profile event. It is also the only version available as part of a collector set (with 191/SV-P Mirror Holo). Higher total supply, but stronger brand recognition.

Grading considerations

Indonesian promo cards were distributed at events without individual packaging — many raw copies show handling wear. PSA 10 pop is relatively low on all versions. Cards from the sealed set (191/SV-P set) tend to grade better. Use BGS or PSA. Avoid non-major grading services for resale value.

Long-term outlook

The PIJ campaign continues in 2025, meaning new variants will expand the series. Collectors seeking complete sets face growing complexity. The cultural angle (UNESCO batik × Pokémon) gives these cards a narrative that transcends the standard TCG market — attracting non-collector buyers.

◆ ◆ ◆

Authenticity warning

Counterfeits of popular Indonesian promos exist. Authentic Pikachu Berkemeja Batik cards carry the "Pikachu's Indonesia Journey" logo in the bottom right of the artwork. Text is entirely in Indonesian. The card stock should match standard Pokémon TCG quality. When in doubt, grade through PSA or BGS before purchasing raw copies at high prices.

Frequently asked questions

Everything
you need to know

Pikachu Berkemeja Batik ("Pikachu Wearing a Batik Shirt") is a series of Indonesia-exclusive Pokémon TCG promotional cards released in 2024. They are part of the official Pikachu's Indonesia Journey campaign, a collaboration between The Pokémon Company and Garuda Indonesia Airlines. Each card features Pikachu wearing a traditional Indonesian batik shirt, with a different motif per city event.

There are 5 official prints from 2024: 101/SV-P (Pikachu + Poké Ball, Bali), 154/SV-P (Xatu, Surabaya), 155/SV-P (Staryu, Yogyakarta), 190/SV-P (Pikachu + Flowers, Jakarta), and 191/SV-P (Mirror Holofoil, part of the Bali collector set). All share the same card mechanics but feature different batik shirt artwork. Additional versions may be released as the campaign continues in 2025.

These cards are only obtainable through the secondary market for buyers outside Indonesia. eBay is the most active marketplace, with multiple Indonesian sellers and international resellers. TCGPlayer also lists them occasionally. Expect to pay a premium — $15–40 for raw copies, $37–102+ for PSA 10 graded. Always verify seller reputation and authenticity markers before purchasing.

Both feature the same Pikachu + Poké Ball batik motif and were distributed at the Bali event. The key difference is the card finish: 101/SV-P is a standard print, while 191/SV-P is a Mirror Holofoil variant. The 191/SV-P was only available as part of the premium "Pikachu Wearing a Batik Shirt Set," which also included a keychain and booster packs of Stellar Guidance. This makes the 191/SV-P rarer and typically more valuable.

These cards have not been officially released in English and their tournament legality varies by region. In Indonesia, they function as standard promo cards within the Indonesian Pokémon Organized Play system. Outside Indonesia, they are primarily treated as collectibles. Consult the official Pokémon Organized Play rules for your specific region before using them in tournaments.

These are Indonesia-exclusive promotional cards intended for the Indonesian market. The Pokédex entry is translated from Japanese into Indonesian — making this one of the few Pokémon cards to feature Indonesian-language text. This linguistic uniqueness is an additional factor in their appeal to multi-language card collectors. There is no official English version of any Pikachu Berkemeja Batik card.

Pikachu's Indonesia Journey is a multi-year Pokémon event campaign operating in Indonesia, extending the global Pokémon Air Adventures project in partnership with Garuda Indonesia Airlines. The campaign includes city-by-city events (Pokémon Run, TCG Academia, Pokémon GO City Safari), a Pikachu Jet aircraft decorated with five batik Pikachu designs, meet-and-greet appearances by Pikachu in batik costume, exclusive merchandise, and the distribution of region-exclusive TCG promo cards. The campaign started in early 2024 and continues through at least 2025.

Several factors support long-term value: geographic exclusivity (never distributed internationally), event-only distribution (no reprint possible without another event), cultural significance (UNESCO batik heritage angle), and the growing interest from non-TCG collectors drawn by the Indonesian identity. However, the campaign's continuation in 2025 increases total supply and may dilute scarcity. PSA 10 copies of the first Bali print (101/SV-P) are likely the most defensible long-term hold.
